Background Information

The Strokes, an iconic American rock band, emerged from the vibrant New York City music scene in the early 2000s, infusing a breath of fresh air into the rock genre. Formed in 1998, the band comprises Julian Casablancas (vocals), Nick Valensi (guitar), Albert Hammond Jr. (guitar), Nikolai Fraiture (bass), and Fabrizio Moretti (drums). Their debut album, "Is This It," released in 2001, received widespread acclaim for its raw, garage-rock sound and Casablancas' distinctive vocal style. The Strokes became the flag bearers of the post-punk revival movement, influencing a new wave of bands and helping to reshape the landscape of rock music. With hits like "Last Nite" and "Someday," The Strokes gained international recognition and established themselves as a seminal force in alternative rock. Their subsequent albums, including "Room on Fire" and "First Impressions of Earth," further solidified their place in the music industry, showcasing a blend of catchy melodies and edgy guitar work. The Strokes' enduring impact on the rock scene has earned them a dedicated fan base and cemented their legacy as one of the defining bands of the early 21st century.

Fun Facts

    Album Cover Art Connection:

  • The iconic cover art for The Strokes' debut album, "Is This It," created quite a buzz. The original cover featured a photograph of a woman's naked behind and hip, taken by Colin Lane. However, due to concerns about the image being too explicit, the cover was altered for the U.S. release. The U.S. version showcased subatomic particle trails instead. The original cover was used for the international release and later for the 2019 reissue of the album.
